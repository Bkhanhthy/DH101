## Reflection

This assignment made me think more carefully about what it means when machines “read”
literature for us. What we gain is speed and pattern recognition. Voyant quickly showed me that
words like “children,” “thousand,” and “number” appear over and over again. Through close
reading alone, I might have noticed the economic language, but I probably wouldn’t have
realized how heavily the essay relies on counting and statistics. Seeing that visually made
Swift’s obsession with numbers feel more intentional and structured. It highlighted how much the
text frames human lives in economic terms.
But what we lose is depth and judgment. Voyant treats frequency as importance, even though
some of the most powerful moments in A Modest Proposal come from shocking imagery or
ironic phrases that don’t repeat often. GPT, on the other hand, confidently explains themes and
satire, but it sometimes smooths over complexity. It can generalize Swift’s critique or miss subtle
historical tensions unless prompted carefully. It sounds authoritative, but it doesn’t truly “know”
when it might be oversimplifying.
Voyant imagines meaning as measurable patterns. GPT imagines meaning as a coherent
explanation. Combining them helped me see both structure and interpretation, but it also made
me realize that human judgment is still necessary. Only a human reader can decide which
patterns actually matter, recognize irony in context, and question whether an interpretation
makes sense. Machines can assist reading, but they cannot replace the responsibility of making
meaning.
